10: Should the United States of America Adopt Universal Healthcare?

America as a country is split with regards to many major polices including : gun control, universal health care and recently, voting rights and immigration. The understanding of the suitability of Universal health care cannot happen in the absence of the context of American psyche and the existing ideas surrounding this policy.

Universal healthcare is a necessity. It should not be a privilege. In the 21st century nearly all of 1st world countries have the money to pay for such programs. America however, is a country that is hailed as the home of capitalism. The economic prosperity and social systems are built to serve the interest of the many companies and have often ignored the principles of welfare and equality.

Often, welfare and minimum wage reforms are seen as charity rather than a necessity for any flourishing society. And the long history of denouncing any thing 'communist / socialist' is a tradition that's held on to today. It is also vital to note the extent of influence of the private insurance companies and lobbyist groups affect the discourse surrounding Universal health care negatively.

Further, high risk groups and those with pre-existing conditions are often left out of many private plans or are provided at very high costs. Effectively, health care is far from the reach of the most vulnerable sections of the American society. Further, the general disapproval of 'federal' influence especially in southern states adds to the growing ideological divide between the contesting groups.

The introduction of President Obama's Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act or better known as Obamacare was a step towards Universal health care. It required everyone to buy a health insurance plan, and those who didn't were taxed. Further, exceptions were even made for those who opted out of health insurance due to financial conditions or religious beliefs.

Some other key aims of PPACA are:

  • Remove the annual and lifetime caps on coverage set by insurers
  • Reduce co-pays as well as remove co-pays on certain services (such as health screenings)
  • limit the ability of insurers to rescind policies
  • prevent sex and age discrimination by insurers
  • set up health-insurance exchanges whereby buyers could easily and readily compare price and coverage
  • require that adult children be insured into their mid 20s as part of family coverage, and offer tax credits to small businesses who provide employees with health insurance
  • making sure that people with pre-existing medical conditions including medical conditions would be insured

While this was a progressives law with many benefits for the majority of society, it drew sharp criticism from the ultra conservative groups who's argument was that it interfered with their personal freedom. This argument barely holds up against the many benefits of UHC, it did manage to stoke the partisan thinking mindset and Obamacare was increasingly seen as part of the 'democratic socialist agenda'. The consequent Trump residency and the dismantling of PPACA are a testimony to this misdirected anger.

With one of the highest costs of medical care, and unequal wealth distribution, America needs to reckon with its reality. The reality of healthcare crisis and the growing inequality between classes. Propaganda and unbacked facts have run rampant regarding UHC. While there might some issues with this model, including waiting times or higher private insurance costs, it is essential that at least the primary care is guaranteed by the state.

This needs American society and beliefs to move away from the propogandist beliefs and look at the real facts and consequences of their votes and decisions. Taxation for UHC is as essential as taxes for infrastructure - eliminating the question of who is going to pay for it? Further, PPACA also identified alternate means of payment for the UHC program.

An objective assessment of the merits of this program need to conducted which that needs unbiased discussions to happen in the Senate, Congress, Supreme court and also in the streets.
